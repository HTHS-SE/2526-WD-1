US GDP Graph

The United States has the largest GDP in the world, driven by its strong service sector, innovation, and consumer spending. The country’s economy benefits from a highly developed financial system, infrastructure, and productivity. Unlike many nations, the US imports more than it exports, reflecting its role as a major consumer in global markets. Its wealth is also supported by diverse industries like healthcare, finance, and technology, which contribute heavily to its growing GDP.


Now let's look into some comparisons between the GDPs of the US and other impactful countries in the world.


GDP Graph of US vs.

China’s GDP is the second largest globally but still around $10 trillion USD less than the US. China is often called the “world’s factory” due to its massive industrial output and manufacturing exports. Unlike the US, China runs a trade surplus, meaning it exports far more than it imports. As you can see in the image, China's GDP has been rapidly growing in recent years, due to an increase in production. However, China is slowly shifting to a more consumer-based economy as production is moving elsewhere around the world.
