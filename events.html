Recession 1973-1975

In 1973, there was an oil crisis in the US due to the oil embargo from the Organization of Arab Petroleum Exporting Countries. Because the oil prices had quadrupled, there was a huge economic shock and a stock market crash. Along with this was a high unemployment rate which all cumulated into the GDP increase rate of the US decreasing.

Recession 1982

Before 1982, the inflation in the country was very high, especially because of the 1979 energy crisis that caused increases in oil prices. Then, in 1982, in order to control the inflation, the Federal Reserve implemented policies of high interest rates, which slowed down economic activity as it was very difficult to borrow money.

The Great Recession 2008

Many factors contributed to the Great Recession in 2008. Many people in the US were mortgaging houses, as they expected the price to keep rising. Then, when the housing prices crashed, many people were paying more for the mortgage than the house actually was worth.

COVID-19 2020

COVID-19 caused a devastating loss for the U.S. economy. It caused the unemployment rate to skyrocket in the U.S. and prices increased. People no longer went outside to buy from restaurants and other services, causing many businesses to be shut down.
